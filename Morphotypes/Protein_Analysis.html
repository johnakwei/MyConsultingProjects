Synopsis

This document is comprised of the following R data analysis items:

  1. Graphs of Protein Data, (with legends), for visualization/understanding the data

  2. Graphs/Tables, (with legends), of the data parameters, (quartiles, etc.)

  3. Hypothesis Tests of Protein Data (correlation coefficients, summary stats, (mean, sd), 95% Confidence)

  4. Notated R Code for future Reproducibility

The dataset examined is: data_protein.csv

The following parameters of data_protein.csv are examined: 1) a - g are the protein signal values (they are not absolute values), but they are relative for each protein separately. They represent the total amount of protein in that sample.

  1. V are the variables measured from each of the 61 samples. v8 - v11 werent done by me and represent fold changes compared to some control.

  2. V3 is known to be bad or hi above 0.7 and is the most important followed by v2 = bad or hi at 7 or 8 and above, and then v1 which is considered to be normal between 0.86 and 1.76 ug/ml. v3 is the ratio of v2/k (potassium).

  3. Previous tried: Fishers exact tests by cross tabulation using hi (0.7) v3 group that contained either hi or lo protein vs the rest of the v3 group < 0.7. Samples hi in v3 are more likely than lo to have hi/lo levels of protein c.

Data Science Goals:
1. To know if my biomarkers (western blot data/signal intensity) can be used to predict elevated levels of some variable.

  1. To find cutoff points for these markers that could indicate good or bad (using a certain amount of signal measured).

  2. Find any meaningful correlations with the proteins of interest (POI), n = 7 columns, that could help predict biological dysfunction.

  3. Develop a methodical approach to analyzing the data so I can answer those 2 questions.
